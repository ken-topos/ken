# Foundation leader overlay

Every catalog work package cites `docs/program/07-catalog-style-guide.md` and
`agent/playbooks/tools/write-ken.md`, and carries readability as an acceptance
axis alongside behavior, proof, trust, and exact checks. The plan reserves a
final exposition pass after proof closure and routes the candidate through an
independent local-naming and formatter-layout review.

The local-binding convention is a judgment rule, not a metric: a name must
state a domain concept, proof endpoint, invariant, or stage otherwise hidden in
nested syntax. Expression length is only evidence; familiar one-step forms,
small direct matches, and direct recursion may be clearer inline. Never
prescribe a binding count, depth threshold, quota, or minimum. Require narrow
scope and unchanged branch/effect order, and treat a long binding chain as a
possible missing helper or lemma.

For two or more sequential local bindings, require one `;`-separated binding
group; keep a one-binding `let` in its already-canonical single-binding form.
